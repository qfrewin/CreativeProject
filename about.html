About

What follows is a short description of my creative project and how I decided what I wanted to do for it.

Goal

Over the course of this semester, I hope to create a website that can help people with large group scheduling.

My Inspiration

I was working as a front desk assosiate at a gymnastics school during the summer of 2020. Soon after starting my job, I learned that scheduling was a nightmare at the facility. The owners first had to ensure that there were enough coaches to teach the classes they wanted to open. They then would have to make sure that class schedules did not interfere with coach availability. Sadly though, that was the easy part.

An hour long class typcically has four different locations they have to visit at the gym. In total, we had about 20 separate locations for the various classes in the gym. At our busiest moments, we could have upwards of 15 classes at a time to work into our schedule. Due to the different natures or levels of the classes, we could not overlap or put two groups into one area at the same time. One of our owners would spend hours each night working on a class schedule for the next day, a task he found very frustrating because a cascade of problems would arise if he messed up even once. I felt there must be a better alternative to the situation, so I started to look for a solution.

Spreadsheets

I first started to look for for a program that the owners could buy that would do everything for them. I searched the internet for a while, but could not find anything that would meet our criteria or that the owners wanted to pay for. Eventually, I gave up looking for something that already existed and instead thought to just make the format of what we had a little more readable in the hopes that the head coach could better plan for the classes and that the coaches would be less confused on where to go or what to do.

The schedule we had at the time was just a plain old spreadsheet, though I did not have much experience working with them. I gave it my best shot though and created a beautifully formatted schedule using Google Sheets (I prefer Google's api over Microsoft 360). During this process, I learned of the power that spreadsheet programs hold. I was introduced to formulas and equations in google sheets, and I thought that maybe instead of looking for a company or program that does what we need, that I could maybe make it myself.

Over the course of a month or so, I hashed together a new spreadsheet that followed the same visual format of the one I created, but that now greatly simplified the scheduling process. After the gym started to implement my spreadsheet, I found myself using more and more of my time on its upkeeping. The owners would continually enter some value in a field where an equation was, or accidentally delete a row and throw off every sheet in the system. The spreadsheet took a load off of the owners, but it made my life a lot busier. I wanted the spreadsheet to be able to do more on its own and not require me to make daily adjustments in order for it to work. I was going to head to college in a few months and I didn't want to recieve a phone call every other day asking me to fix it. After some more research, I found Google Apps Script.

Programming

Google apps script was my first venture into programming. I had never done anything with programming before, and never thought I would. I really had no idea what I was doing, but I found several snippets of code on stack overflow or other people's blogs that I would hash together into a sort-a "Frankenstine" of a code. I was eventually able to make my spreadsheet account for processes such as adding a new coach or class into the system and reapplying formulas when an editor messed them up.

It took me until just a few days before I left for college, but I finally was able to get my spreadsheet to a place where I would not have to fix it every day while in school. Although crude and all hard-coded, I was proud of the work I did. I fell in love with programming and decided it was something I wanted to pursue as a career and study it in college.

My Plan

During my time in school, I learned a lot more about programming. Although I am still proud of the work I did for the gymnastics school, I know I can now do better. I have a lot of ideas of new features to add to it and how to just make it more efficient overall. One of the problems with Google sheets is that there are so many ways people can mess with the spreadsheet that I just can't account for. There are also dozens of editting and formatting options that I just don't need the user to see or worry about.

My hope is that during CS 260 to learn enough to implement my program on my own website. I would love to have a login page where different users and different gyms or businesses could potentially logon and store their own information and have options to customize the program to their needs. Who knows, if I can actually make it work, I may consider selling the program. I know my goal is maybe ambitious, but I hope to learn enough this semester to make it happen.
